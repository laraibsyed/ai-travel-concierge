# 👵 GRANDMA.md

Hi Grandma!

You know how you might call a travel agent and ask them to help plan a holiday?
This app does that, but on a computer, and it never sleeps.

## What does it actually do?

You type in something like *"I want to spend 3 days in Paris without spending too much money"*
and it writes you a little plan, what to see, what kind of places to eat at, that sort of thing.

## Is it just making things up?

This is the important bit. Most AI chatbots you've heard about (like ChatGPT) will answer
any question using everything they've ever been "taught", which means they can sometimes
guess or make things up confidently.

This app is different. Before it answers you, it first goes and looks things up in a folder
of notes I wrote myself about 7 specific cities. It can **only** use what's in those notes.
So if you ask it about Melbourne, it won't pretend to know — it'll tell you honestly that
it doesn't have that information.

Think of it like a travel agent who only specialises in 7 destinations, and refuses to
recommend anywhere they haven't personally visited.

## What are the 7 places it knows about?

Dubai, Tokyo, Paris, London, New York, Madrid, and Baku.

## What happens if you try to trick it?

I tested this! I asked it for a chocolate chip cookie recipe. It said no and reminded me
it's a travel planner, not a chef. It stays focused on its job.

## How did you build it?

I wrote the code, used some AI tools to help me with the boring repetitive bits
(like a spell checker, but for code), checked everything worked properly, and then
put it on the internet so anyone can use it, not just on my laptop.

## Does it cost money to run?

Not at all! It uses Groq which is a free model for the conversational bit!
